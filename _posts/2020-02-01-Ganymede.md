Ganymede is the seventh satellite and the third Galilean moon of Jupiter, which is also the largest and most massive moon in the solar system. 
Ganymede is the only Galilean moon of Jupiter named after a male figure—like Io, Europa, and Callisto, he was a lover of Zeus.

## Subsurface ocean

Although there are rumours that in the 1970s NASA scientists have already suspected that Ganymede has a thick ocean between two layers of ice, one on the surface and one beneath a liquid ocean and atop the rocky mantle, it was not until the era of Galileo mission in the 1990s that Ganymede's subsurface ocean was confirmed.
